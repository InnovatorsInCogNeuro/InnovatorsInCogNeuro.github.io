About us

Elevating diverse and innovative ideas

Using science for social justice.

Our mission

The Innovators in Cognitive Neuroscience Symposia welcomes thought-leaders exploring the neural mechanisms that give rise to thought, knowledge, and complex behavior. Even as novel technologies, methods, and analytical tools proliferate, we believe it is the diverse and creative individuals within the neuroscience community that are most critical for moving our understanding of the brain forward. We are dedicated to curating and disseminating innovative theories, as well as a forum for cultivating new ideas.

At the same time, we recognize that long-standing injustice and inequality pervade our society, with academia is no exception. We think it is time for academics to take an preeminant role in the movement towards social justice and work actively towards building a more just and equitable society. As scientists, we think that our academic endeavors should reflect this aim.

The Innovators in Cognitive Neuroscience bridges these scientific and social justice aspirations. Our speakers are offered the opportunity to present their ground-breaking research. To lead off each seminar, we ask that our speakers highlight a social justice cause or charity to raise awareness, and audience members may consider donating to these causes, if they are in a position and able to do so. In addition, The Innovators in Cognitive Neuroscience Seminar Series is actively working to elevate careers of under-represented groups in academia through our choice of speakers. To ensure that this aim is fulfilled, the seminar series will self audit and report the demographics and identities of our speakers, with an eye towards both transparency and equity.

Who are innovators?

Innovators in cognitive neuroscience are leaders in the field who push the boundaries of current methodological and theoretical frameworks to advance our understanding of the brain. Being an inherently multidisciplinary enterprise, innovators come to neuroscience from a variety of academic backgrounds, including cognitive psychology, computer science, and sociology. Innovators may be working in academia, clinical settings, government, or industry.

Importantly, underrepresented groups have been among the greatest innovators in academic research, but historically these individuals have not been fully recognized for their contributions. The Innovators in Cognitive Neuroscience Symposia is committed to amplifying the voices of innovators from historically underrepresented groups, and to cultivating a just and equal environment in academia.

Commitment to social justice through science

Scientific research does not take place in a vacuum, and we believe that it is crucial for scientists to take a lead in creating a just and equal society. To this end, we encourage our speakers to highlight a social justice charity of their choice at the beginning of their talk to raise awareness.
